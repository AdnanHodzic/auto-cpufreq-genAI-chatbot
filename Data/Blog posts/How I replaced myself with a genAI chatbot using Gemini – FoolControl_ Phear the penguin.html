How I replaced myself with a genAI chatbot using Gemini

Adnan Hodzic January 16, 2025
0
0

It’s been 5 years since I created auto-cpufreq. Today, it has over 6000 stars on GitHub, attracting 97 contributors, releasing 47 versions, and reaching what I conservatively estimate to be at least 30000 users. With such a broad user base comes a significant volume of (support) questions. Most of these are repetitive, questions that have already been answered and documented somewhere, and which you could find if you knew where to look.

But realistically speaking, most people don’t want to sift through GitHub issues or documentation to find the answers they need. Even I sometimes struggle to find things Ive written myself. Instead, people want immediate answers. As the creator of this project, I have the most knowledge about it, which has effectively turned me into an “answering machine.”. This got so tedious that I even created a FAQ template to copy-paste responses.

This got me thinking: how could I remove myself from this equation? The answer was clear, build a genAI auto-cpufreq chatbot, capable of answering questions just as I would, and that’s exactly what I did!

auto-cpufreq genAI chatbot demo & showcase Youtube video:

For those who didn’t watch the YouTube video above where I explain everything:

You can ask the auto-cpufreq genAI chatbot any questions you’re looking for an answer to. Please note that it’s still far from perfect and doesn’t know everything I do about auto-cpufreq. Also, every conversation and interaction will be logged! So, please thumbs up or down the answers you receive, as Ill periodically review the conversation history to improve things.

Until next time, happy hacking!
